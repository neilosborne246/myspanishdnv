The My Spanish DNV blog covers all aspects of Spain's Digital Nomad Visa — income and financial requirements, the UGE application process, tax implications including Beckham Law, life in Spain as a remote worker, and country-specific guides for applicants from the UK, UAE, US, Australia, and beyond.
Articles are reviewed and updated regularly as Spanish immigration rules, income thresholds, and processing times change. The income requirement figure — currently €2,849/month for 2026 — is updated each time the Spanish government revises the SMI. Each article shows a published and last-updated date.
Articles are written by the My Spanish DNV team and reviewed in partnership with Platinum Legal Spain, a regulated Spanish law firm based in Spain. Our writers work directly with immigration lawyers to ensure the information reflects current practice — not just the written rules.
Blog articles are written to be accurate and useful, but they are general information only — not legal advice for your specific circumstances. Immigration law can change, and individual situations vary significantly. For a personal assessment of your eligibility, start your application and speak to a case manager.
The minimum income requirement for Spain's Digital Nomad Visa in 2026 is €2,849 per month. This equals 200% of Spain's 2026 Salario Mínimo Interprofesional (SMI). For each additional family member included in the application, an extra €1,069/month is required. See our dedicated income requirements article for a full breakdown of what counts as qualifying income.
Via the UGE route (applying from within Spain), processing typically takes approximately 20 working days — roughly 4 calendar weeks. Total time from starting your case to having your TIE permit card in hand is typically 10–12 weeks, once document preparation, UGE processing, and your TIE appointment are all factored in. See our week-by-week timeline article for the full breakdown.
