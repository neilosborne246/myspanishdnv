Is a call right for you?

Who should book a consultation call

A call is most useful when you have a specific question, a complex situation, or you are simply not sure whether you are eligible. Here are the situations where a call is most valuable.

  • ✓ You are unsure whether your income meets the €2,849/month minimum — for example, your income is variable, comes from multiple sources, or is partly in a non-EUR currency.
  • ✓ You are employed and your employer has not yet confirmed they will permit remote work from Spain, or you are unsure whether your employment contract supports this.
  • ✓ You have a complex situation — a previous Spanish visa, a criminal record question (including historic or spent convictions), or a past residency issue in Spain or another EU country.
  • ✓ You want to understand Beckham Law before applying — whether you are likely to qualify and how the tax savings compare to standard IRPF rates for your income level.
  • ✓ You are planning a family application and want to understand the income threshold uplift and document requirements for your family size.
  • ✓ You have already had an application rejected and want to understand whether an appeal or a fresh application is the better path.
  • ✓ You are self-employed with multiple clients across different countries and want to confirm your income structure meets the DNV criteria.
  • ✓ You are close to your 90-day visa-free entry window and want urgent advice on whether you can still submit in time.

What to expect

What happens on the call

The call is structured to give you maximum value in 20 minutes. We start with your situation, confirm eligibility, explain the process, and answer your specific questions — in that order.

First 5 minutes

Your situation

We ask about your income (amount and source), employment or self-employment structure, nationality, current country of residence, and whether you are planning to include family members. This gives us the context to answer your questions accurately.

Minutes 5–15

Eligibility confirmation & process overview

We confirm whether you meet the requirements, explain the recommended application route (usually UGE), walk through what documents you will need and which to start first (the criminal record certificate), and give you a realistic timeline and cost overview.

Final 5 minutes

Your questions

Open Q&A for anything we have not covered — Beckham Law, family income thresholds, what happens after approval, how the dashboard works. If a question requires detailed legal advice, we note it and follow up by email or recommend a specific specialist.
