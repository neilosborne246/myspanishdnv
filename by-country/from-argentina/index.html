Argentine applicants

Argentine nationals — DNV or Spanish citizenship?

Argentina has a very large Spanish diaspora, and many Argentines are eligible for Spanish citizenship through grandparents (Ley de Nietos / Ley de Memoria Democrática). If you are eligible for Spanish citizenship, you do not need the DNV — you would register as a Spanish citizen directly. For those without Spanish ancestry, Spain's DNV is the most straightforward legal route to long-term Spanish residency.

Check first

Do you qualify for Spanish citizenship?

Before applying for the DNV, Argentine nationals should check whether they qualify for Spanish citizenship through their family history. Spain's Ley de Memoria Democrática (formerly Ley de Nietos) grants citizenship to grandchildren and great-grandchildren of Spanish citizens who fled during the Civil War or Franco era. If you qualify, Spanish citizenship is a stronger status than the DNV — and it's worth investigating before starting a DNV application.

Schengen access

Argentine passport — Schengen visa-free

Argentine passport holders have Schengen visa-free access for up to 90 days in any 180-day period. For DNV applicants, this means the UGE route is fully available: fly to Spain (Buenos Aires to Madrid is approximately 12–13 hours), apply via UGE, and receive your 3-year permit without returning to Argentina. Buenos Aires-Madrid direct flights operate with Iberia and LATAM.

Argentine peso income and the exchange rate

Argentina's complex exchange rate environment means that demonstrating €2,849/month in EUR or USD terms is straightforward for Argentine professionals working for international clients — most are paid in USD or EUR. For those whose income is primarily in ARS, the informal/official rate gap requires careful consideration. Your case manager will advise on how to evidence income correctly in your specific situation.

Criminal record certificate

Police certificate requirements for Argentine nationals

All DNV applicants must provide a criminal record certificate apostilled or legalised for international use. For Argentine nationals, the required certificate is the Certificado de Antecedentes Penales.

Apply

Registro Nacional de Reincidencia (RNR) — apply online at argentina.gob.ar

Apply online at argentina.gob.ar/justicia/reincidencia for a Certificado de Antecedentes Penales. Processing takes 5–10 business days. The certificate can be requested from abroad by Argentine nationals through the online portal. No in-person visit to Argentina is typically required.

Apostille / Legalisation

Certifying for use in Spain

Argentina is a member of the Hague Convention. Apostilles on Argentine documents are issued by the Ministerio de Relaciones Exteriores y Culto (Cancillería Argentina). Apply online via the Cancillería website or in person. Allow 5–10 business days. The apostilled certificate is valid for use in Spain.

Start early

Allow 2–3 weeks total (RNR certificate + Cancillería apostille)

The police certificate is frequently the longest lead-time document in a DNV application. Apply for it as early as possible — your case manager will advise on optimal timing to avoid delays to your submission.

Living in Spain

Popular cities and practical tips for Argentine nationals in Spain

Barcelona has the largest Argentine expat community in Spain and is overwhelmingly the most popular city for Argentine DNV applicants. Madrid is popular for finance and media professionals. Málaga and Valencia are growing in popularity. Buenos Aires and Barcelona have a long cultural exchange — Argentine tango, theatre, and food culture are well established in Barcelona.

Tax planning

Tax implications for Argentine nationals

Argentina taxes its residents on worldwide income. Once Spanish tax resident (183+ days in Spain), you file Spanish IRPF or Beckham Law returns. Argentina's current economic situation means many Argentine professionals earning in USD will find the EUR conversion process managed by their case manager. The Spain-Argentina double taxation agreement prevents double taxation.
