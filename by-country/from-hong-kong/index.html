Hong Kong applicants

Hong Kong professionals and Spain's DNV

A significant number of Hong Kong professionals have been exploring international options since 2020. Spain's DNV, combined with Beckham Law's 24% flat rate and EU residency access, makes it an attractive option. Hong Kong has a Spanish Consulate General, making local application straightforward. HKSAR passport holders require a Schengen visa to enter Spain, so the consulate route is typically most practical.

Consulate in Hong Kong

Spanish Consulate General Hong Kong

The Spanish Consulate General in Hong Kong handles DNV applications for Hong Kong residents. This is the primary route for HKSAR passport holders, as they require a Schengen visa to enter Spain (making the UGE route an extra step). Consulate processing typically takes 2–3 months. British National (Overseas) (BN(O)) passport holders should verify their specific Schengen access before considering the UGE route.

Tax advantage

Hong Kong's low tax vs Beckham Law

Hong Kong's standard income tax rate (salaries tax) is capped at 15%. Spain's Beckham Law at 24% flat is higher — but comes with EU residency, European access, and the full Mediterranean lifestyle package. For Hong Kong professionals whose effective tax rate exceeds 15% (e.g. through complex income structures), the comparison changes. The Spain-Hong Kong bilateral arrangements should be reviewed with a tax specialist.

BN(O) passport holders — check your Schengen access

British National (Overseas) (BN(O)) passport holders may have different rights depending on visa status in third countries. If you hold both a HKSAR passport and a BN(O), clarify which passport you plan to travel on and what Schengen access that confers. Your case manager will advise on the optimal route for your specific passport situation.

Criminal record certificate

Police certificate requirements for Hong Kong nationals

All DNV applicants must provide a criminal record certificate apostilled or legalised for international use. For Hong Kong nationals, the required certificate is the Certificate of No Criminal Conviction.

Apply

Hong Kong Police Force (HKPF) — apply online at hkpf.gov.hk

Apply for a Certificate of No Criminal Conviction via the Hong Kong Police Force online portal (hkpf.gov.hk). You will need a valid Hong Kong identity card or passport. Processing typically takes 10–15 working days. If outside Hong Kong, apply through the relevant authority with certified copies of your ID documents.

Apostille / Legalisation

Certifying for use in Spain

Hong Kong documents can be authenticated by the Apostille Authority in Hong Kong (under the Secretary for Justice's office). Allow 5–10 working days. The apostilled certificate is required for use in your Spanish DNV application.

Start early

Allow 3–5 weeks total (HKPF certificate + apostille)

The police certificate is frequently the longest lead-time document in a DNV application. Apply for it as early as possible — your case manager will advise on optimal timing to avoid delays to your submission.

Living in Spain

Popular cities and practical tips for Hong Kong nationals in Spain

Barcelona is the most popular destination for Hong Kong DNV applicants — the international community, cosmopolitan lifestyle, and European access appeal strongly. Madrid attracts finance professionals. Valencia and Málaga are popular for a lower-cost coastal lifestyle.

Tax planning

Tax implications for Hong Kong nationals

Hong Kong levies salaries tax at up to 15% (or a standard rate of 15% on net income, whichever is lower). Once Spanish tax resident, you pay Spanish IRPF or Beckham Law (24% flat). Hong Kong does not impose a formal exit tax. The bilateral arrangements between Spain and Hong Kong should be reviewed with a specialist tax adviser before your move.

Practical

Banking and finances

Cathay Pacific, Qatar Airways, and Emirates connect Hong Kong to Madrid and Barcelona. Hong Kong time (HKT, UTC+8) is 6–7 hours ahead of Spain — manageable for European client-facing roles.
