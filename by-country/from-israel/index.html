The Israeli applicant situation

Israeli tech and finance professionals choosing Spain

Spain is an increasingly popular destination for Israeli professionals — the Mediterranean climate, cultural connections, and Beckham Law's 24% flat tax rate for high earners make it compelling. Israel's Schengen visa-free status means the fast UGE route is available. Direct flights from Tel Aviv (Ben Gurion Airport) to Barcelona and Madrid make the move logistically simple.

Mediterranean connection

Tel Aviv to Barcelona — familiar culture, different coast

Israel and Spain share Mediterranean geography, climate, and cultural temperament. Israeli tech professionals, artists, and entrepreneurs find Barcelona and Madrid culturally accessible. Barcelona is under 4 hours from Tel Aviv by Vueling, EasyJet, or Iberia. Hebrew-speaking Israeli communities have established themselves in Barcelona, Madrid, and other Spanish cities.

Tax advantage

Beckham Law for Israeli high earners

Israel's income tax reaches 50% at the top rate (47% national + 3% surtax on high incomes). Spain's Beckham Law offers qualifying first-time Spanish tax residents a flat 24% income tax rate for up to 6 years. For Israeli tech founders, senior developers, and finance professionals with high incomes, the saving is very significant. The Spain-Israel tax treaty prevents double taxation.

🚀

Start-up founders and equity holders — plan carefully

If you have Israeli company equity, stock options, or a pending M&A transaction, the timing of your Spanish tax residency matters enormously for how those proceeds are taxed. Israel taxes capital gains; Spain's Beckham Law has specific rules on foreign-source capital gains. Engage a Spain-Israel cross-border tax specialist before making the move — ideally well before the liquidity event.

Application routes

Consulate or UGE — the two routes to Spain's DNV

Israel passport holders are Schengen visa-free — you can enter Spain for up to 90 days and apply via UGE from within Spain. This is the fastest route and avoids consulate appointments in Israel entirely. You may also apply at Spanish Embassy Tel Aviv before travelling to Spain.

Criminal record certificate

Police certificate requirements for Israeli nationals

All DNV applicants must provide a criminal record certificate apostilled or legalised for international use. For Israeli nationals, the required certificate is the Certificate of Good Conduct (תעודת יושר).

Apply

Israel Police (משטרת ישראל) — apply via the government services portal

Apply for a Certificate of Good Conduct through the Israeli government services portal (gov.il) or in person at Israel Police stations. Processing typically takes 7–14 business days. If outside Israel, apply through the Israeli Embassy or Consulate in your country of residence.

Apostille / Legalisation

Certifying for use in Spain

Israel is a member of the Hague Convention (since 1977). Apostilles on Israeli official documents are issued by the Ministry of Justice (משרד המשפטים). Apply at the Ministry of Justice offices or through an accredited agent. Allow 5–10 business days.

Start early

Allow 3–4 weeks total (police certificate + Ministry of Justice apostille)

The police certificate is frequently the longest lead-time document in a DNV application. Apply for it as early as possible — your case manager will advise on optimal timing to avoid delays to your submission.

Living in Spain

Popular cities and practical tips for Israeli nationals in Spain

Barcelona is the top choice for Israeli DNV holders — the Mediterranean vibe, beach culture, and established Israeli expat community make it feel accessible. Madrid is popular for finance professionals. Málaga is growing in popularity for its lower cost and outdoor lifestyle. There are established Israeli communities and Hebrew-speaking services in Barcelona's Eixample and Gràcia.

Tax planning

Tax implications for Israeli nationals

Israel taxes its residents on worldwide income at rates up to 50%. When you become Spanish tax resident (183+ days in Spain), your Israeli tax residency ceases. Israeli-source income (Israeli company dividends, Israeli rental income) may still attract Israeli withholding tax. The Spain-Israel double taxation treaty prevents double taxation. Beckham Law at 24% is available for qualifying first-time Spanish tax residents — apply within 6 months of registration.

Practical

Banking and finances

Multiple daily flights connect Tel Aviv (TLV) to Barcelona and Madrid via Vueling, EasyJet, Iberia, and El Al. Israel time (IDT/IST, UTC+2/+3) is 0–1 hours ahead of Spain — an almost identical time zone, making Spain ideal for Israeli professionals maintaining Israeli client relationships.
