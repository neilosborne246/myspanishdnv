Application routes

Consulate or UGE — the two routes to Spain's DNV

Kuwait passport holders require a Schengen tourist/visit visa to enter Spain. This means the UGE route requires an extra step — obtain a Schengen visa first, then enter Spain and apply. Most applicants from Kuwait find the consulate route more straightforward. Apply at the Spanish Embassy in your country in Kuwait before travelling to Spain.

Criminal record certificate

Police certificate requirements for Kuwait nationals

All DNV applicants must provide a criminal record certificate apostilled or legalised for international use. For Kuwait nationals, the required certificate is the Kuwaiti Criminal Record Certificate (شهادة حسن السيرة والسلوك).

Apply

relevant national authority

Apply for a criminal record certificate (Good Conduct Certificate / شهادة حسن السيرة والسلوك) at the Ministry of Interior Criminal Investigation Department (CID) in Kuwait City. Applications can be made in person or through the MOI e-services portal. Fee: nominal.

Apostille / Legalisation

Certifying for use in Spain

1. Obtain certificate from MOI CID. 2. Legalise at the Ministry of Foreign Affairs (Attestation Section), Salmiya, Kuwait. 3. Present to the Spanish Embassy in Kuwait City for consular attestation. Bring originals and photocopies at each stage.

Start early

Allow MOI certificate: 3–5 working days. MFA legalisation: 5–7 working days. Spanish Embassy attestation: 1–2 weeks. Sworn Spanish translation: 5–10 days. Allow 8–10 weeks total.

The police certificate is frequently the longest lead-time document in a DNV application. Apply for it as early as possible — your case manager will advise on optimal timing to avoid delays to your submission.

Living in Spain

Popular cities and practical tips for Kuwait nationals in Spain

Madrid and Barcelona are the most popular destinations for Gulf nationals in Spain, with strong Arabic-speaking communities. Marbella (Costa del Sol) has a long-established Gulf Arab presence. Ibiza and Palma are favoured for shorter stays. Valencia offers excellent value with a Mediterranean coastal lifestyle.

Tax planning

Tax implications for Kuwait nationals

Once you become Spanish tax resident (183+ days in Spain in a calendar year), you file IRPF in Spain. A double taxation treaty between Spain and Kuwait prevents being taxed on the same income twice. Specialist cross-border tax advice is recommended before making the move.

Practical

Banking and finances

Kuwaiti nationals in Spain's Gulf-facing real estate and investment sectors have been a constant presence for decades. Halal food is widely available in major Spanish cities. Ensure your income documentation demonstrates non-Spanish sources. Private health insurance covering Spain is mandatory for the DNV application.
