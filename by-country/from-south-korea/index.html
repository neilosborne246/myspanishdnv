The Korean applicant situation

South Korean professionals and Spain's DNV

Spain's DNV is attracting South Korean tech workers, game developers, content creators, and professionals who want a European base. Korea's strong remote work culture, high salaries, and growing interest in Mediterranean lifestyle make the DNV a natural fit. Korea's Schengen visa-free status means the fast UGE route is available — no consulate appointment needed, just fly to Spain and apply.

Schengen access

Korean passport — Schengen visa-free

Korean Republic (South Korean) passport holders can enter the Schengen area visa-free for up to 90 days in any 180-day period. This makes the UGE route fully available: fly to Spain (Seoul to Madrid is approximately 13–14 hours via connections), apply for the DNV from within Spain, and receive your 3-year residence permit without returning to Korea during the process.

Tax advantage

Beckham Law vs Korean income tax

South Korea's national income tax reaches 45% at the top rate, plus 10% local income tax surtax. Spain's Beckham Law allows qualifying first-time Spanish tax residents to pay a flat 24% income tax on Spanish-source income for up to 6 years. For Korean tech professionals, game developers, and finance workers, the tax saving is significant. Spain and South Korea have a double taxation treaty preventing the same income from being taxed twice.

Korean certificate is fast — apostille is the key step

The Korean NPA criminal record certificate can be issued within 1–5 business days, making it one of the fastest in Asia. The key timeline item is the MOFA apostille — allow 3–7 business days. Total turnaround is typically under 2 weeks, making the Korean police certificate one of the quicker documents in a DNV application.

Criminal record certificate

Police certificate requirements for South Korean nationals

All DNV applicants must provide a criminal record certificate apostilled or legalised for international use. For South Korean nationals, the required certificate is the Criminal Record Certificate (범죄경력조회 회보서).

Apply

National Police Agency (경찰청) via local police station or overseas via Korean Embassy

Apply in person at any police station in South Korea or at the Korean Embassy/Consulate in your country of residence if already abroad. Processing is typically 1–5 business days. The certificate must then be apostilled by the Ministry of Foreign Affairs.

Apostille / Legalisation

Certifying for use in Spain

South Korea is a Hague Convention member. Apostilles on Korean documents are issued by the Ministry of Foreign Affairs (외교부). Apply at the MOFA apostille service or through an accredited agent. Allow 3–7 business days.

Start early

Allow 1–2 weeks total (police certificate + MOFA apostille)

The police certificate is frequently the longest lead-time document in a DNV application. Apply for it as early as possible — your case manager will advise on optimal timing to avoid delays to your submission.

Living in Spain

Popular cities and practical tips for South Korean nationals in Spain

Barcelona is the most popular city for Korean DNV applicants — its design culture, food scene, and Mediterranean lifestyle appeal strongly. Madrid is popular for Korean professionals in business and finance. Valencia is growing in popularity for its lower cost and beach lifestyle. Korean communities are developing in Barcelona's Eixample and Gràcia districts.

Tax planning

Tax implications for South Korean nationals

South Korea taxes its residents on worldwide income. Once you establish Spanish tax residency, you cease to be a Korean tax resident going forward. Korean-source income may still be subject to Korean withholding tax. The Spain-South Korea double taxation treaty prevents double taxation. Beckham Law at 24% is available for qualifying first-time Spanish tax residents — apply within 6 months of registering as a Spanish tax resident.

Practical

Banking and finances

Seoul and Incheon have connecting flights to Madrid and Barcelona via major Middle Eastern and European hubs. Korean time (KST, UTC+9) is 7–8 hours ahead of Spain. Korean professionals working for European clients find the time zone from Spain very manageable. Korean banks generally allow non-resident accounts — notify your bank once you become Spanish tax resident.
