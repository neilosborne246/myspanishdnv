What Is the DNV

Spain's visa for remote workers and freelancers

Introduced in 2023, the Digital Nomad Visa (DNV) is a Spanish residency permit that allows non-EU nationals to live in Spain while working remotely for employers or clients based outside the country.

🌍

Live anywhere in Spain

Barcelona, Madrid, Valencia, Málaga — choose your base and stay legally for up to 3 years, renewable for 2 more.

💻

Keep working remotely

You can work for your existing employer or clients abroad. Up to 20% of your income can come from Spanish sources.

💰

Optional Beckham Law tax benefit

Elect to pay a flat 20% tax on Spanish income for the first 6 years instead of the standard progressive rates up to 47%.

Requirements

Who qualifies for the Spain DNV?

You need to meet several conditions. The core requirements are income, employment type, and clean background. Here's what matters most.

01

Remote work for non-Spanish employer

You must work for a company or clients based outside Spain. Up to 20% of your income can come from Spanish sources without affecting eligibility.

02

Minimum monthly income

You need to demonstrate a stable income of at least 200% of the Spanish minimum wage — approximately €2,500/month. Higher for family applications.

03

Proof of employment or clients

Employees need a contract and employer letter. Freelancers need to show active client contracts with at least 1 year of working relationship.

04

Private health insurance

You need a Spanish private health insurance policy with full coverage during your stay. This must be from an insurer registered in Spain.

05

Clean criminal record

You'll need to submit a criminal background check from every country you've lived in over the last 5 years, apostilled and translated if required.

06

Non-EU nationality

The DNV is for non-EU/EEA citizens. If you're from an EU country, you have the right to live and work in Spain without a visa.

How It Works

The application process, step by step

There are two application routes — from your home country (consulate) or from within Spain. We guide you through whichever applies to you.

1

Initial consultation and eligibility check

We review your situation — income, employment type, country of residence — and confirm you qualify. If anything needs adjusting, we'll tell you now, not after you've submitted.

2

Document preparation

We produce your full document checklist, draft your employer/client letters, advise on translations and apostilles, and prepare your application forms. Nothing gets missed.

3

Health insurance

You'll need a Spanish private health insurance policy before you apply. We can connect you with Sanitas plans specifically designed for DNV applicants via our partner site.

4

Application submission

We submit your application through the correct channel — Spanish consulate in your home country, or the UGE immigration unit if you're already in Spain on a short-stay visa.

5

Approval and arrival

Once approved, you collect your visa and travel to Spain. Within 30 days of arrival you register for your TIE (residence card) — we handle this too.
