Timing

When should you start your renewal?

Spanish immigration law allows you to submit a renewal up to 90 days before your permit expires. Do not leave it until the last minute — document collection and preparation take time, and a lapsed permit is a serious complication.

90
Days before expiry

Open your renewal case — ideal window

90 days out is the ideal moment to open your renewal dashboard case. This gives you comfortable time to gather updated documents, resolve any queries, and submit well ahead of expiry with no pressure.

60
Days before expiry

Latest comfortable start point

60 days is the minimum comfortable window. Document preparation typically takes 1–2 weeks; submission and processing around 20 working days. Starting here leaves little buffer for unexpected delays.

30
Days before expiry

Submitted — awaiting decision

If you submitted before expiry, you remain in legal status while your renewal is pending — even if the decision comes after your current permit date. This is known as prórroga de estancia.

Do not let this pass without submitting

If your permit lapses without a submitted renewal, you may be out of legal status. Recovery is possible but significantly more complex and costly. Contact us immediately if you are approaching expiry without a renewal in progress.

Requirements

What do you need to renew your Spain DNV?

The renewal requirements broadly mirror the original application — you must still meet the core eligibility criteria and demonstrate ongoing remote working activity. Here is what our team will check and prepare.

💰

Continued income — €2,849/month minimum

You must still demonstrate at least €2,849/month (200% of the 2026 SMI) from remote work. Recent payslips or invoices covering the previous 3 months are required.

💼

Updated employment or client contract

An updated employment contract (remote employees) or current client invoices and contracts (freelancers/autónomos) confirming your continued remote working arrangement.

🏠

Proof of residence in Spain

Evidence that you have been residing in Spain during your permit period — typically a rental or purchase contract, utility bills, or padrón municipal certificate.

📋

Clean criminal record

An updated criminal record certificate from your home country, apostilled. This must typically be issued within 3 months of your renewal submission date.

🏥

Private health insurance (if employed)

Updated proof of valid, Spain-compliant health insurance coverage. We recommend spanish-healthinsurance.com or 247expatinsurance.com.

🛂

Valid passport

Your passport must be valid for the duration of the renewal period. If your passport is due to expire, renew it before starting the visa renewal process.

ℹ️

Family member renewals

Dependent family members included in your original application must also renew at the same time. The primary applicant's income must still meet the combined threshold: €2,849 base + approximately €1,068 for a spouse + €356 per child. A family discount applies on our renewal fee. The Long View

Your path to permanent residency in Spain

Every renewal brings you closer. After 5 years of continuous residence in Spain, you can apply for long-term permanent residency — and eventually Spanish citizenship after 10 years.

Yr 1

Initial Permit

UGE: 3-year permit. Consulate: 1-year visa → TIE registration.

Yr 3

First Renewal

2-year renewal. This is where you are now — renew to keep your residency and reach 5 years.

Yr 5

Second Renewal

Another 2-year renewal — or apply for long-term permanent residency if you meet the criteria.

Yr 5+

Permanent Residency

After 5 years of continuous legal residence you can apply for long-term residency — no more renewals.

Yr 10

Spanish Citizenship

Most nationalities can apply for Spanish citizenship after 10 years of legal residence.
